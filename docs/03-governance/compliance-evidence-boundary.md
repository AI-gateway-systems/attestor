# Compliance Evidence Boundary

Status: common boundary for F9 compliance documentation.

Attestor's compliance posture is evidence-support, not certification. The
repository can produce engineering anchors and reviewable artifacts. It cannot
replace an auditor, customer control owner, legal review, live deployment proof,
or operating organization.

## Evidence Types Attestor Can Produce

- signed release and admission artifacts
- evidence packs and proof refs
- audit remediation tracker entries
- research provenance ledger entries
- policy activation and shadow promotion records
- replay/idempotency records
- data-minimization and secret-safe output test evidence
- production rehearsal packets
- architecture and package-surface tests

## Evidence Types Still Needed Outside The Repo

- auditor sampling plan and audit period evidence
- customer access-review records
- HR onboarding/offboarding records
- vendor due-diligence approvals
- live backup and restore drill results
- live incident records and postmortems
- regional deployment and data-transfer evidence
- DPA/subprocessor/legal notices
- independent penetration-test report

## Procurement Positioning

Safe wording:

> Attestor is built with SOC 2, ISO/IEC 27001, and ISO/IEC 42001 evidence
> needs in mind, and ships repository-side engineering anchors for those reviews.

Unsafe wording:

> Attestor is SOC 2 or ISO compliant out of the box.
